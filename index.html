Parallel and Perpendicular Lines Math Lib Quiz

Directions: Utilizing what you know about the slopes of parallel and perpendicular lines, answer the following multiple choice questions. Use your answers to fill in the math lib at the end of the quiz to reveal a silly story.

If you get stuck, use the following as a resource:

  1. Write the equation of a line passing through (4, -9) and parallel to the line 3x-6y=30.

    • A. y=2x+1 (Mr. Boyd)
    • B. y=1/2x+1 (Ms. Clarke)
    • C. y=1/2x-11 (Mrs. Jimenez)
    • D. y=-2x-1 (Mr. Perjuste)
    • E. y=-2x-9 (Ms.Holmes)

  2. Write the equation of a line passing through (-4, -11) and perpendicular to the line 4x+5y=-45.

    • A. y=5/4x-16 (excited)
    • B. y=5/4x-6 (confused)
    • C. y=4/5x+1 (shocked)
    • D. y=4/5x+5 (annoyed)
    • E. y=-4/5x-8 (embarrassed)

  3. Write the equation of a line passing through (-1, 3) and parallel to line AB with A(3, -5) and B(-2, 15).

    • A. y=-4x-1 (eating Hot Cheetos)
    • B. y=-4x+7 (snorkeling)
    • C. y=-4x-3 (making bacon pancakes)
    • D. y=1/4x+5 (playing mini golf)
    • E. y=1/4x-8 (skydiving)

  4. Which line is perpendicular to the line passing through the points (-4, 2) and (4, -4)?

    • A. 3x+4y=16 (Drake)
    • B. 3x-4y=4 (Beyonce)
    • C. 4x+3y=15 (Bad Bunny)
    • D. 4x-3y=9 (Taylor Swift)
    • E. 3x+4y=-4 (Kanye)
